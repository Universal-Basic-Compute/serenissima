# Strategy Framework for Serenissima AI Player

This document provides structured decision-making frameworks for different aspects of gameplay, enabling systematic evaluation of options and strategic planning.

## Investment Decision Framework

### Property Investment Criteria
- **ROI Threshold**: Minimum acceptable annual return percentage
  - Premium Locations: 5-7%
  - Standard Locations: 8-10%
  - Developing Areas: 12-15%

- **Capital Allocation Limits**:
  - No single property > 40% of liquid assets
  - Total property investments < 70% of net worth
  - Maintenance reserve of 10% of property value

- **Strategic Value Factors**:
  - Location synergy with existing properties
  - Vertical integration with business operations
  - Growth potential of district
  - Diversification benefits
  - Social prestige considerations

### Business Investment Criteria
- **ROI Threshold**: Minimum acceptable annual return percentage
  - Core Industries: 15-20%
  - New Ventures: 25-30%
  - Expansions: 18-22%

- **Capital Allocation Limits**:
  - No single business > 30% of liquid assets
  - Total business investments < 60% of net worth
  - Operating reserve of 20% of annual expenses

- **Strategic Value Factors**:
  - Supply chain integration
  - Market share potential
  - Barrier to entry advantages
  - Skill and knowledge alignment
  - Risk diversification benefits

### Trade Investment Criteria
- **ROI Threshold**: Minimum acceptable return percentage
  - Low-risk trades: 10-15%
  - Medium-risk trades: 20-25%
  - High-risk trades: 35%+

- **Capital Allocation Limits**:
  - No single trade > 20% of liquid assets
  - Total trade positions < 50% of liquid assets
  - Diversification across at least 3 goods

- **Strategic Value Factors**:
  - Market intelligence advantage
  - Relationship leverage
  - Seasonal opportunity
  - Supply chain support
  - Competitor impact

## Market Analysis Framework

### Price Trend Evaluation
- **Trend Identification**:
  - Rising: 3+ consecutive price increases
  - Falling: 3+ consecutive price decreases
  - Volatile: Alternating increases and decreases
  - Stable: Price changes < 5%

- **Causal Analysis**:
  - Supply factors (production changes, import disruptions)
  - Demand factors (seasonal changes, new uses)
  - Regulatory impacts (taxes, restrictions)
  - Competitor actions (hoarding, dumping)

- **Response Options**:
  - Rising Market: Sell existing inventory, increase production
  - Falling Market: Buy for inventory, reduce production
  - Volatile Market: Hedge positions, trade actively
  - Stable Market: Focus on efficiency and margins

### Opportunity Identification
- **Supply-Demand Gaps**:
  - Shortage indicators (rising prices, depleted inventories)
  - Surplus indicators (falling prices, accumulated inventories)
  - Seasonal predictors (historical patterns)
  - Geographic arbitrage (price differences between locations)

- **Evaluation Metrics**:
  - Potential profit margin
  - Time window duration
  - Capital requirement
  - Risk factors
  - Competitive advantage

- **Prioritization Factors**:
  - Alignment with strategic focus
  - Resource availability
  - Implementation speed
  - Sustainability of advantage
  - Relationship leverage

## Competitive Strategy Framework

### Competitor Analysis
- **Capability Assessment**:
  - Financial resources
  - Property holdings
  - Business operations
  - Relationship networks
  - Political influence

- **Strategic Orientation**:
  - Primary focus areas
  - Risk tolerance
  - Growth patterns
  - Collaborative vs. competitive tendency
  - Innovation approach

- **Vulnerability Identification**:
  - Resource constraints
  - Overextension indicators
  - Relationship weaknesses
  - Market position threats
  - Regulatory exposure

### Competitive Positioning
- **Direct Competition Approach**:
  - Avoid: When competitor has overwhelming advantage
  - Challenge: When you have specific advantages
  - Collaborate: When mutual benefits exceed competitive gains
  - Ignore: When markets are sufficiently distinct

- **Market Position Strategies**:
  - Differentiation: Quality, service, or unique offerings
  - Cost Leadership: Efficiency and scale advantages
  - Focus: Specialized expertise in niche areas
  - Integration: Control of multiple supply chain elements

- **Relationship Tactics**:
  - Alliance building with complementary players
  - Neutral stance with powerful competitors
  - Defensive positioning against aggressive players
  - Opportunistic collaboration on specific ventures

## Risk Management Framework

### Risk Assessment
- **Risk Categories**:
  - Market risks (price volatility, demand changes)
  - Operational risks (production problems, property issues)
  - Political risks (regulatory changes, faction conflicts)
  - Relationship risks (partner reliability, competitor actions)

- **Impact Evaluation**:
  - Financial impact scale (minor to severe)
  - Reputation impact scale (limited to critical)
  - Strategic impact scale (negligible to threatening)

- **Probability Estimation**:
  - Low: <10% chance
  - Moderate: 10-30% chance
  - High: 30-60% chance
  - Very High: >60% chance

### Risk Response
- **Mitigation Strategies**:
  - Diversification across markets and activities
  - Insurance and hedging mechanisms
  - Relationship development as social insurance
  - Information advantage cultivation
  - Flexibility preservation

- **Contingency Planning**:
  - Reserve requirements (15-20% of operating capital)
  - Alternative supplier relationships
  - Market pivot capabilities
  - Crisis response protocols

- **Opportunity Risks**:
  - Experimental allocations (5-10% of resources)
  - Staged implementation of risky ventures
  - Early warning indicators for rapid adjustment
  - Learning integration from small failures

## Implementation Process

When making significant decisions:

1. **Information Gathering**
   - Collect relevant market data
   - Assess current resource availability
   - Analyze competitive landscape
   - Consider social and political factors

2. **Option Generation**
   - Identify at least three alternative approaches
   - Consider both conventional and innovative options
   - Include status quo as a baseline comparison
   - Evaluate different time horizons

3. **Systematic Evaluation**
   - Apply relevant frameworks from above
   - Quantify factors where possible
   - Consider qualitative factors systematically
   - Assess alignment with overall strategy

4. **Decision Execution**
   - Implement decisions with appropriate resources
   - Establish monitoring mechanisms
   - Set review points for evaluation
   - Maintain flexibility for adjustments

5. **Learning Integration**
   - Document decision outcomes
   - Analyze deviations from expectations
   - Identify improvement opportunities
   - Update decision frameworks based on experience
   
## Building Investment Framework

### Location Evaluation Criteria
- **Rent Levels**: Assessing existing rent amounts on potential lands
  - Premium districts: High rents but also high income potential
  - Established areas: Moderate rents with steady income flows  
  - Developing zones: Lower rents but more uncertainty

- **Growth Prospects**: Anticipating future value increases
  - Upcoming infrastructure improvements
  - Planned developments by other citizens
  - Shifting geographic desirability patterns

- **Existing Building Mix**: Considering current property utilization
  - Complementary building types (synergies)
  - Competing buildings (saturation risks)
  - Opportunities to differentiate offerings

- **Strategic Positioning**: Aligning with overall property strategy
  - Geographic clustering for efficiency
  - Diversification across districts
  - Integration with business operations
  - Social prestige and visibility

### Building Selection Criteria
- **Income Potential**: Evaluating revenue generation
  - Rent levels for different building types
  - Occupancy and tenant quality expectations
  - Operating costs and maintenance requirements
  - Profit margin projections

- **Capital Requirements**: Upfront and ongoing costs
  - Construction costs for building type
  - Financing costs if using debt capital
  - Ongoing maintenance and staffing expenses
  - Reserve funds for repairs and renovations

- **Opportunity Costs**: What else the capital could achieve
  - Alternative property investments
  - Business expansion possibilities
  - Trade and inventory investments
  - Maintaining flexibility for future options

- **Non-Financial Factors**: Qualitative considerations
  - Vertical integration benefits
  - Diversification into new sectors
  - Social prestige and status associated with building
  - Strategic control or monopolistic potential
  
### Implementation Plan
- **Construction Timeline**: Project planning and execution
  - Permitting and approval processes
  - Contractor selection and agreements
  - Material sourcing and delivery schedules
  - Monitoring and quality control oversight

- **Operating Model**: Determining utilization approach
  - Self-operation vs. leasing to tenants
  - If leasing, tenant screening criteria
  - If operating, staffing and management plans
  - Marketing and promotions for occupancy

- **Financial Structuring**: Funding the investment
  - Capital reserves allocated
  - Financing amount and terms if using debt
  - Projecting ongoing cash flows
  - Return on investment calculations

- **Review Cycle**: Tracking performance and re-evaluating
  - Occupancy and income monitoring
  - Expense management and efficiency reviews
  - Market position reassessment
  - Holding period and exit planning
  
## Lease Management Framework 

### Lease Profitability Analysis
- **Building Income**: Evaluating revenue generation
  - Rent amounts for different tenant types
  - Occupancy and tenant quality expectations  
  - Ancillary income opportunities (services, fees)
  - Profit margin projections

- **Building Expenses**: Ongoing costs to consider  
  - Maintenance requirements and staffing costs
  - Taxes and fees paid to government
  - Financing costs for any loans
  - Reserves for repairs and renovations
  
- **Net Operating Income**: Income minus expenses
  - Calculate NOI for each building
  - Compare to market benchmarks for that area
  - Identify underperforming and outperforming assets

### Fair Market Lease Rates
- **Comparable Properties**: Researching similar buildings
  - Building type (residential, commercial, industrial)
  - Age, condition and amenities of the property
  - Location within Venice (premium vs. developing area)
  - Tenant mix and overall occupancy levels

- **Risk Factors**: Adjusting for specific circumstances
  - Credit risk of tenants (secured corporate vs. individuals)
  - Term of lease (longer commitments reduce risk)
  - Responsibility for taxes, insurance and maintenance  
  - Potential for future rent increases

- **Lease Structures**: Considering different formats
  - Flat rental rate for entire lease term
  - Periodic escalations (annual increases)
  - Percentage rent (based on tenant revenues)
  - Combination structures with base plus percentage

### Lease Optimization Approach
- **Income Maximization**: For buildings you own outright
  - Charge highest supportable market rents  
  - Implement percentage rent where applicable
  - Aggressively increase rents upon renewals
  - Prioritize high-quality, creditworthy tenants

- **Expense Minimization**: For buildings on leased land
  - Negotiate rents as low as market will allow
  - Extend lease terms for cost certainty  
  - Seek favorable tax and maintenance responsibilities
  - Leverage relationships and negotiate firmly

- **Strategic Considerations**: Beyond just financials
  - Tenant mix and property utilization  
  - Vertical integration opportunities
  - Potential property redevelopment plays
  - Social prestige and visibility benefits

### Implementation Plan  
- **Data Collection**: Gathering relevant information
  - Details on all current leases (rates, terms, responsibilities)
  - Comparable property rental data from the market
  - Operating data for each property (income, expenses)
  - Strategic priorities for that asset

- **Analysis Process**: Evaluating each property  
  - Calculate current and market rental rates
  - Assess under/over market status of existing leases  
  - Identify leases requiring adjustment
  - Prioritize lease changes based on financial impact

- **Negotiation Plan**: Preparing for lease discussions
  - Establish target rental rates and lease terms
  - Identify negotiation leverage points
  - Develop contingency positions  
  - Align with other strategic initiatives

- **Execution**: Implementing the new leases
  - Conduct lease renegotiations or renewals  
  - Update lease documentation and records
  - Transition to new rates and terms
  - Monitor for successful implementation

- **Performance Tracking**: Ongoing management  
  - Track income and expense performance
  - Identify additional lease adjustments needed
  - Integrate learnings into future lease decisions
  - Refine optimization process based on results
