## Market Influence Map

### Luxury Goods Market
- **Dominant Players**:
  - Sofia Zanchi: ~15%, Strong Eastern connections and Venetian elite clientele
  - Marco Venier: ~10%, Prestigious glassmaking for European nobility  
  - Various Venetian Nobles: ~40%, Traditional control of high-end market
  - Italian Mainland Producers: ~25%, Specialized craftsmanship from Florence, Milan

- **Market Dynamics**:
  - Power Balance: Competitive
  - Barrier to Entry: High (requires connections, reputation, quality)
  - Price Control: Dominated by established Venetian merchants
  - Quality Standards: Guild-regulated with strong emphasis on provenance

- **Strategic Implications**:
  - Entry Strategy: Partnership with established Venetian merchants (Sofia, Marco)
  - Alliance Opportunities: Craftsmen guilds, mainland Italian producers
  - Competitive Approach: Differentiation through unique mainland-Venetian synthesis
  - Risk Factors: Quality control across distributed production, reputation management

### Metallurgy/Blacksmith Market
- **Dominant Players**:
  - Venetian Arsenal: ~30%, Military and maritime focus
  - Guild-Affiliated Shops: ~50%, Traditional products and services
  - Independent Specialists: ~20%, Niche products and innovations

- **Market Dynamics**:
  - Power Balance: Concentrated among guild-affiliated operations
  - Barrier to Entry: Moderate (requires skills, equipment, guild relationships)
  - Price Control: Guild-influenced with some flexibility for specialists
  - Quality Standards: Strictly regulated by metalworkers guild

- **Strategic Implications**:
  - Entry Strategy: Differentiate with mainland Italian techniques
  - Alliance Opportunities: Specialized craftsmen, material suppliers
  - Competitive Approach: Focus on unique mainland Italian metallurgy traditions
  - Risk Factors: Guild resistance, establishing reputation in Venetian market
