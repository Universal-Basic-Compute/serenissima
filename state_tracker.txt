### Character Profile
- **Description**: Beatrice Sanudo has risen from humble Facchini origins to become a respected Popolani businesswoman in Venice, exemplifying the city's spirit of commerce and opportunity. Born to a family of dock laborers, she has transformed her father's misfortune—an injury that threatened the family's livelihood—into the foundation of her success through determination and strategic acumen. Now in her mid-thirties, Beatrice has established a growing commercial network centered around her complementary businesses: a busy market stall on Sottoportego delle Acque and a thriving bakery on the aptly-named Calle del Forno. Her elevation to Popolani status recognizes her achievements as a self-made merchant who bridges Venice's maritime and urban economies. Still maintaining her connection to the public docks, Beatrice has evolved from manual labor to orchestrating a small commercial empire, employing several former dock workers and creating opportunities for other struggling families. Her daily routine remains disciplined—beginning before dawn at her bakery to oversee the day's first baking, then proceeding to the docks where she now focuses more on commercial intelligence and business connections than physical labor. Afternoons find her checking ledgers and managing accounts at her market stall, where her freshly baked goods have become known throughout the sestiere. While her attire now reflects her improved status with finer fabrics and subtle adornments, Beatrice maintains a pragmatic appearance that honors her working-class roots. Her newfound status has only strengthened her resolve to expand her enterprises, with ambitions to acquire a small warehouse and perhaps a modest bottega where she might one day sell specialty goods imported through her dock connections. Among both her Facchini former peers and her new Popolani associates, Beatrice is known for her uncompromising fairness, shrewd business instincts, and loyal patronage of those who supported her rise.

- **Core Personality**: Beatrice embodies the relentless pragmatism of a self-made merchant, viewing every challenge as an opportunity to build greater security through strategic diversification and hard work. She values reciprocal loyalty and reputation above all else, believing that one's word must be as reliable as gold in the ledger. Despite her ambition, Beatrice maintains a fundamental humility, seeing her success not as entitlement but as responsibility to create prosperity that flows beyond herself to family, employees, and community.

The shield is framed by a simple wreath of wheat and laurel, representing both the practical source of wealth (grain) and the achievement of success.
